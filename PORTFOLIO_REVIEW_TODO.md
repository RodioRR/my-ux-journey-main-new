## 1. Copy & Language

- [ ] **Trim buzzwords** — 12 rotating adjectives feels scattered. Consider 5–6 that are distinct and memorable (e.g. *curious*, *asking the right questions*, *a UX Researcher*, *multilingual*, *taking ownership*). Fewer = stronger.
- [ ] **Add a short value line** — After the name, add one clear sentence that states what you do and why it matters. Example: *"I turn user research into decisions that move the needle."* Keeps it simple and concrete.
- [ ] **"UX Research Portfolio" label** — Fine as-is. Optional: *"Portfolio"* or *"UX Researcher"* for a cleaner first impression.

### About
- [ ] **Fix grammar** — *"The passion for asking users the right questions **are** rooted"* → *"**is** rooted"*. Also: *"the ability to lead between the lines"* is unclear; consider *"reading between the lines"* or rephrase.
- [ ] **Tighten opening** — *"I'm a curiosity-driven UX Researcher taking ownership and initiative"* is a bit generic. Add one concrete outcome: e.g. *"I turn messy data into clear recommendations."*
- [ ] **Add a short story** — One 1–2 sentence anecdote (e.g. a project where research changed a decision) would add a storytelling element without bloat.
- [ ] **"Research that moves the needle"** — Strong headline. Keep it.

### Contact
- [ ] **Sharpen the CTA** — *"Whether you need a UX research strategy, user testing, or data-driven insights — I'd love to hear about your project"* is good but slightly passive. Consider: *"If you're building something and want research that drives decisions, let's talk."*
- [ ] **"Send a Message"** — Clear. Alternative: *"Get in Touch"* or *"Start a conversation"* for a warmer tone.

### Case Studies
- [ ] **Lead with impact** — In subtitles, lead with the outcome when possible (e.g. *"+5 p.p. cart conversion — how removing one checkout step changed the funnel"*).
- [ ] **Shorten scopeOfOwnership** — Some are very long. Consider a 1–2 sentence summary for the card, with full detail in the expanded view.
- [ ] **Tone down "Key Learnings"** — Phrases like *"Excelled in bridging"* and *"honed my skills"* can sound self-promotional. Use neutral, factual language: *"Bridged UX research with product strategy"* instead of *"Excelled in bridging."*
- [ ] **"What Could Be Done Differently"** — Good for credibility. Keep it, but keep it brief.

### Testimonials
- [ ] **Add English translation for Polish quote** — Maciej’s testimonial is in Polish. Add a short English version or a translated excerpt so international recruiters can read it.
- [ ] **Fix typo** — *"I have pleasure"* → *"I had the pleasure"* (Kamil’s quote).
